```
Q: What do you mean by cloud-native?
A: Cloud-native refers to development and validation environments that leverage cloud infrastructure, virtualization, and scalable computing resources.
This enables to develop automotive software more efficiently.

Q: Why are you focusing on cloud-native development?
A: SDV software is becoming increasingly complex, and traditional development environments are reaching their limits.
Cloud-native environments improve scalability, collaboration, and development efficiency.

Q: Why investigate industries outside automotive?
A: Similar challenges exist in smartphone, semiconductor, and robotics development.  
We would like to learn from successful cloud-native practices in these industries.  

Q: Why is thermal simulation important?
A: Thermal issues directly affect reliability, safety, and system performance.  
Early thermal validation can reduce development risks before physical prototypes are available.  

Q: Are you looking at software validation only?
A: No. We are interested in both software and hardware validation.  
This includes thermal simulation, power analysis, and system-level verification.  

Q: Why did you select QNX Cabin?
A: QNX Cabin is a widely used automotive platform with a strong ecosystem.  
It provides a good example of virtualized automotive development environments.  

Q: Why did you select PAVE360?
A: PAVE360 is one of the leading digital twin platforms for automotive development.
It supports comprehensive system-level validation in virtual environments.

Q: What kinds of virtual platforms are you interested in?
A: We are interested in virtual ECUs, virtual SoCs, digital twins, and cloud-hosted development environments.   
These technologies help accelerate development and validation activities.

Q: Are you interested in on-premises solutions?
A: Yes. We would like to compare SaaS, on-premises, and hybrid deployment models.  
Understanding the advantages and disadvantages of each approach is important.

Q: What is the expected outcome of this research?
A: We would like to create a comparison table covering key solutions and technologies.  
This information will support future technology evaluation and decision-making.

Q: Are AI-enabled tools included in the scope?
A: Yes. We are interested in AI-assisted development and validation capabilities.  
Examples include automated testing, simulation optimization, and code generation.

Q: What adoption information are you looking for?
A: We would like to understand real-world adoption examples and production use cases.
Customer references and case studies are especially valuable.

Q: What is the highest priority area of this research?
A: Virtual platforms and cloud-based hardware validation are our highest priorities.
In particular, we would like to investigate thermal simulation technologies in depth.

Q: How do you define success for this research?
A: Success means identifying leading solutions and understanding their capabilities.
We also want to create a structured comparison that supports future planning.

Q: Why are you interested in smartphone development use cases?
A: Smartphone development has adopted virtual devices and cloud-based validation earlier than automotive development.  
These experiences may provide useful insights for SDV development.

Q: Are you interested in autonomous driving development as well?
A: Yes. Autonomous driving systems require large-scale simulation and validation.
As a result, cloud-native development approaches are widely adopted in this area.

Q: Why is cloud-based validation becoming important?
A: Physical testing alone is becoming increasingly expensive and time-consuming.
Cloud-based validation enables large-scale testing and faster development cycles.

Q: What kind of hardware validation capabilities are you looking for?
A: We are interested in thermal simulation, power analysis, and performance evaluation.
We would also like to understand how these functions are integrated into cloud-native workflows.

Q: What is the main benefit of virtual platforms?  
A: Virtual platforms allow development to start before physical hardware is available.  
This can significantly reduce development time and cost.
```
